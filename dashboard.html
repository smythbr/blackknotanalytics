Core Assets

SPY, GLD, and BIL are not a simplification — they are a conclusion. Markowitz established in 1952 that portfolio risk is governed by the correlations between assets, not their individual volatilities. Tobin's Separation Theorem extended this: the question is not which risky assets to hold, but how much to allocate to the risk-free asset. PRISM is a dynamic implementation of that theorem. Three structurally orthogonal return drivers — equity premium, monetary hedge, risk-free carry — span the efficient frontier without redundancy across every economic environment: growth, recession, inflation, deflation. The discipline is in the simplicity.

SPY
The Market Portfolio
The equity risk premium is the most durable and well-documented return source in finance. Fama and French confirmed that market beta explains the majority of equity returns across asset classes and geographies. SPY is the purest, most liquid, and most cost-efficient expression of that premium available to any investor in the world.
GLD
From Complement to Core
For four decades, bonds diversified through a specific macro regime — declining inflation, falling rates. In 2022 that regime ended: the S&P 500 fell 18% while the Bloomberg Aggregate fell 13% simultaneously — the worst year for a 60/40 since 1937. Gold carries no duration risk, no credit risk, and no dependence on central bank credibility. Under Basel III, allocated physical gold is a Level 1 HQLA — on equal regulatory footing with cash.
BIL
Capital in Reserve
Not dead money. Deployed defensively when PRISM signals stress, earning positive carry throughout, positioned for decisive redeployment when conditions clear. When PRISM returns to Risk-On, preserved capital re-enters at the earliest stages of recovery — the most powerful part of the compounding cycle — rather than from a position of impairment.
Rider Pool

The rider pool was constructed through an exhaustive, pre-specified quantitative process applied to a broad universe of global ETFs spanning sector, factor, geographic, and style dimensions. Each candidate was evaluated against risk-adjusted return persistence, drawdown profile, correlation to the core portfolio, and a turnover penalty designed to favour structurally persistent over episodically elevated momentum.

The surviving pool is deliberately small. Each instrument expresses a distinct, identifiable economic theme with its own return drivers, correlation structure, and macro sensitivity — ensuring that the pool itself is diversified at the level of economic exposure, not merely at the level of ticker selection.

XOP and KBE passed through every filter. They are complementary — energy and regional banking represent distinct macro transmission channels. When credit conditions tighten, energy spreads and bank margins often diverge. When macro conditions are genuinely favourable, both tend to exhibit persistent momentum simultaneously, allowing VAMS to confirm a high-confidence signal. When only one qualifies, it reflects a sector-specific opportunity. When neither qualifies, SPY absorbs the allocation — the model does not force a rider when evidence is absent.

XOP
Energy — Macro Transmission
S&P Oil & Gas Exploration & Production. Energy responds directly to global demand, credit conditions, and dollar strength. Energy spreads diverge early in credit stress and lead on momentum when macro conditions are genuinely favourable — distinct return drivers from the core portfolio, distinct transmission channel from KBE.
KBE
Financials — Credit Cycle
S&P Regional Banks. Bank net interest margins track the yield curve and credit cycle — a distinct transmission channel from energy. The separation from XOP is deliberate: concurrent strength in both signals high-confidence macro support; divergence keeps the rider dormant. Independent economic themes, not merely different tickers.

Frequently Asked Questions
Everything you need to understand AEGIS, act on the signal, and evaluate the evidence.
What is this service, and what does AEGIS do?+
BLACK KNOT ANALYTICS is an independent educational service built around AEGIS — a systematic, rules-based macro investment model designed for the sophisticated DIY investor. AEGIS monitors market conditions continuously, classifies the current market regime, and generates a daily signal specifying the portfolio allocation to hold across three core ETFs.

Every decision the system makes is evidence-driven and fully systematic. There is no discretion, no opinion, and no human override in the signal generation process. This is not a fund, an advisory service, or a managed account. It is an educational resource that publishes a research signal.
Why SPY, GLD, and BIL specifically?+
Each asset plays a specific, non-redundant role.

SPY provides broad, liquid equity exposure at minimal cost — the cleanest expression of the equity risk premium available to any investor.

GLD provides genuine diversification across both inflationary and risk-off environments. Unlike bonds, gold carries no duration risk. It does not decline alongside equities when interest rates rise — the failure mode that made traditional 60/40 portfolios so destructive in 2022.

BIL provides pure capital preservation while earning positive carry during defensive periods. Capital held in T-bills is not idle — it is positioned for decisive redeployment the moment conditions clear.

This three-asset structure deliberately replaces bonds with T-bills to eliminate the scenario where equities and bonds fall simultaneously. 2022 was the defining illustration of why that distinction matters.
How does the system generate its signals?+
AEGIS applies a proprietary multi-signal stress detection framework that synthesises several structurally independent market data streams into a single, continuous measure of market stress — an indicator of whether conditions favour risk-taking or capital preservation.

When stress is low — a Risk-On regime — the portfolio is fully deployed into risk assets. As stress rises toward Risk-Off, exposure is reduced systematically, shifting capital toward defensive positions. A tactical Rider overlay may further enhance returns during Risk-On conditions. When conditions deteriorate, the Rider goes dormant.

The specific signal inputs, weighting methodology, and tactical mechanics are proprietary. Operationally, what matters is straightforward: when the signal indicates a rebalance, you rebalance. When it says hold, you hold.
How do I act on the signal?+
The signal specifies exactly what allocation to hold — the target percentage weight for each asset in the portfolio. When the dashboard shows “No Action Required,” hold your current allocation. When it shows “Action Required,” rebalance to the new target weights.

Execution is a simple ETF trade through your existing brokerage account. One decision, made on average fewer than 18 times per year.
Will I be automatically notified when a rebalance is required?+
Not currently. AEGIS does not send push notifications, email alerts, or SMS messages when the signal changes. Monitoring the dashboard is the subscriber’s responsibility.

The practical approach is to make it part of your daily routine — a brief check at the start of each trading day takes seconds. The Today’s Signal panel displays the current regime and whether any action is required. When the signal has changed, it will show “Action Required” alongside the updated target weights.

Regime-driven rebalances are not time-sensitive to the minute, but acting within the trading day is generally appropriate. A transition that occurs overnight will be visible from market open.
What is a regime-driven rebalance?+
A regime-driven rebalance is triggered by the AEGIS signal. It occurs when the system’s stress measure crosses a defined threshold, changing its classification of the current market environment — for example, from Risk-On to Neutral, or from Neutral to Risk-Off.

When the dashboard shows “Action Required,” that is a regime-driven rebalance. Acting on it promptly is the subscriber’s core implementation task. These are the rebalances the service is built to deliver.
What is a drift rebalance, and who is responsible for it?+
Over time, actual portfolio weights drift away from their targets as individual ETF prices move. After a sustained equity rally, for example, your SPY allocation may creep well above its target weight. A drift rebalance restores the portfolio to target — there is no change in the AEGIS regime classification driving it.

AEGIS does not generate drift rebalance alerts. Monitoring for drift and deciding when to correct it is the subscriber’s responsibility. A common approach is to review allocation quarterly and rebalance when any individual position has drifted more than three to five percentage points from its target.

The system handles regime changes. You handle drift.
How often should I expect to rebalance?+
Regime-driven rebalances average fewer than 18 per year across the full evaluation period — roughly once every three weeks. Activity clusters around genuine stress events, so the system is quiet during sustained bull markets and more active during periods of real dislocation.

Drift rebalances are additional and depend on how closely you monitor your allocation. For most subscribers, a quarterly drift review is sufficient.

Every rebalance — regime-driven or drift-correcting — is a simple ETF trade through your existing broker.
How does the Rider work?+
The Rider is a tactical overlay that becomes active when AEGIS is in a Risk-On regime. It represents a portion of the total portfolio allocated not to SPY, but to a single sector ETF that the system has identified as delivering statistically meaningful, risk-adjusted outperformance relative to the broad market.

When the Rider is active, the Today’s Signal panel will show the assigned sector ETF alongside the target weights for SPY, GLD, and BIL. When no sector candidate meets the required performance threshold, the Rider allocation reverts to SPY — you will see SPY absorb that weight in the displayed targets.

The Rider is completely dormant during Neutral and Risk-Off regimes.
Is the Rider always the same asset?+
No. The system evaluates a pool of candidate sector ETFs — currently XOP (energy) and KBE (regional banks) — and deploys whichever is delivering the strongest, most persistent risk-adjusted outperformance relative to the broader market under the current regime.

Once a Rider is deployed, it is not displaced by a challenger that merely performs marginally better. A meaningful performance gap must be established before a switch occurs. This prevents unnecessary churn and keeps transaction costs reasonable.

The Rider may therefore hold its position for extended periods, or rotate when the system detects a genuine, sustained shift in relative sector performance. When a switch occurs, the dashboard will show “Action Required” with updated target weights. The composition of the Rider pool is reviewed annually.
What should I expect when the signal goes Risk-Off?+
A Risk-Off signal means the system has detected elevated market stress and reduced equity exposure materially, shifting capital into defensive positions. The portfolio is designed to preserve capital during these periods, not pursue returns.

You should expect to participate less — or not at all — in equity market rallies while Risk-Off is active. That is the known, accepted cost of the strategy. The system has occasionally moved defensive in markets that continued higher. That is not a malfunction; it is the deliberate trade-off that makes deep-drawdown protection possible.

When conditions clear and the signal transitions back toward Risk-On, positions are rebuilt progressively. The single most important discipline is to follow the signal consistently — especially when doing so feels most difficult.
Will AEGIS outperform the market every year?+
No — and that is by design.

AEGIS is explicitly structured to prioritise capital protection over full upside capture. In years where markets rise strongly and stress remains low throughout, a simple buy-and-hold S&P 500 position will likely outperform. 2019 and 2024 are examples from the evaluation period where AEGIS lagged SPY — in both cases, a measured posture limited participation in portions of the rally.

This is the honest cost of a drawdown-first strategy. It is paid back many times over in years like 2020 and 2022, where protecting capital during the decline preserved the base from which recovery compounds.

The system is not designed to win every year. It is designed to win over the full cycle.
How does the system’s creator personally use AEGIS?+
AEGIS manages my entire personal investment portfolio — not a subset of it. I run no separate satellite allocation.

When building into a position — transitioning from Risk-Off back toward Risk-On, for example — I do not deploy the full target allocation in a single trade. I dollar-cost average into the position gradually, spreading entry across several transactions. This limits the risk of committing full capital at the wrong moment during a recovery that may not yet be confirmed.

When reducing risk — a shift toward Neutral or Risk-Off — I act quickly and decisively. The position is reduced in full, not partially. The asymmetry is intentional: gradual entry when rebuilding, fast exit when protecting.

This is how I personally implement the signal. Subscribers will make their own decisions about execution based on their circumstances.
What about taxes?+
Each regime-driven rebalance may constitute a taxable event in non-registered accounts, as positions are sold and capital gains may be realised.

AEGIS is most efficiently deployed inside tax-advantaged structures — RRSPs and TFSAs in Canada, IRAs and 401(k)s in the United States — where rebalancing events carry no immediate tax consequence and every dollar of return compounds without friction.

For taxable accounts, the relevant question is not tax deferral versus realisation in isolation. It is whether the capital preserved by moving defensively outweighs the tax cost of doing so. In a year like 2022, that comparison is unambiguous.

This platform does not provide tax advice. Consult a qualified tax professional for guidance specific to your situation, jurisdiction, and account structure.

What do GIPS conformance and BarclayHedge CTA standards mean?+
GIPS (Global Investment Performance Standards) are the globally recognised performance reporting standards developed by the CFA Institute. They govern how returns must be calculated, presented, and disclosed.

BarclayHedge CTA standards are the reporting requirements applied to managed futures and systematic trading advisors — the institutional benchmark against which strategies like AEGIS are assessed.

Conforming to both means the performance record was calculated using the same methodology required of institutional asset managers: no cherry-picked time periods, no selective presentation of results, full disclosure of methodology. It is the standard of accountability the institutional industry demands, applied to a retail implementation.
